YCI Bangladesh successfully implemented the "Chaya Manchitro" (Shadow Map) project at the University of Dhaka campus today. Supported by dedicated YCI volunteers, this initiative utilized a manual sensory approach to identify the campus’s most critical "Heat Spots" and "Cool Zones." ​From the Central Library to the VC Chattar, our teams examined peak sun hours to firsthand map the stark temperature contrasts between exposed areas and shaded sanctuaries. ​To share these findings, we strategically installed handcrafted "Campus Heat Maps" and awareness posters at high-traffic locations, including Hakim Chattar, the FSSS Compound, the Shadow area, and the Arts Faculty. Each poster features a QR Code inviting the campus community to explore the data and recognize the urgent need for more green spaces, such as "Bot-Tolas," to reduce our carbon footprint. ​By pinpointing specific areas for urgent tree plantation, we are moving beyond mere observation toward actionable solutions for a cooler, greener campus.
