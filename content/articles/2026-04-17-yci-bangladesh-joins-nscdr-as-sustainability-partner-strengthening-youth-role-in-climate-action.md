YCI Bangladesh has proudly announced its participation as a Sustainability Partner in the upcoming **National Seminar on Climate and Disaster Resilience (NSCDR)**, reinforcing its commitment to advancing youth engagement in sustainability, climate action, and disaster preparedness across Bangladesh.

The seminar is being organized by the Institute of Disaster Management and Vulnerability Studies under the University of Dhaka, one of the country’s leading academic institutions. The event is being held in collaboration with several prominent national and international organizations, including the Center for DMERT, Friendship, CDD, SAWAB, ASB, and German Cooperation.

The NSCDR is expected to serve as a vital national platform, bringing together a diverse group of stakeholders, including academics, researchers, policymakers, development practitioners, and youth leaders. The seminar will focus on facilitating meaningful, evidence-based discussions around some of the most pressing challenges facing Bangladesh today—particularly climate change, disaster risk reduction (DRR), and long-term resilience building.

Participants will engage in knowledge sharing, policy dialogue, and collaborative discussions aimed at identifying innovative and sustainable solutions to mitigate climate-related risks. With Bangladesh being one of the most climate-vulnerable countries in the world, such initiatives are crucial in strengthening institutional capacity and promoting community-based resilience strategies.

The inclusion of YCI Bangladesh as a Sustainability Partner highlights the increasing recognition of youth as key stakeholders in climate governance and sustainable development. Through its involvement, YCI Bangladesh aims to amplify youth voices, encourage civic engagement, and contribute to shaping actionable strategies that align with national and global sustainability goals.

By participating in this significant national event, YCI Bangladesh continues to demonstrate its dedication to fostering a more resilient, informed, and proactive generation—capable of addressing the evolving challenges of climate change and disaster management in Bangladesh.
